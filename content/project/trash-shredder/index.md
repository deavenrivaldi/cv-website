## **Overview**
This project focuses on designing a mechanical system to reduce landfill waste volume through shredding and compaction. It was inspired by the increasing waste management challenges in Jakarta, where landfill capacity is approaching its limit.

> Reduces waste volume and improves landfill efficiency through integrated mechanical processing.
---

## **Problem**
- Rapid growth of waste volume leading to landfill overflow
- Risk of environmental hazards such as waste avalanches
- Inefficient storage of untreated solid waste

---

## **Solution**
I designed a combined **dual-shaft shredder and piston-based compactor** to process waste into smaller, dense blocks that are easier to store and manage.

---

## **System Design**
### Mechanical Design
- Dual rotating shafts for shredding solid waste
- Piston mechanism for compressing shredded material
- Flap system to contain waste during compaction and release output

### Structure
- Enclosed casing to ensure safe and controlled operation
- Modular component design for assembly and maintenance

### Tools & Technologies
Autodesk Inventor (3D modeling and assembly design)

---

## **Key Features**
- Integrated shredding and compaction in a single system
- Improved waste density for efficient storage
- Mechanically simple and scalable design

---

## **Outcome**
- Produced complete 3D component and assembly models
- Demonstrated feasibility of compact waste processing system
