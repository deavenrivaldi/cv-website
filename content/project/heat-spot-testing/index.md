## **Overview**
This project was developed as my undergraduate thesis in collaboration with an industrial partner. The goal was to improve the safety and efficiency of heat spot testing processes used in quality control environment

> Eliminates human exposure to hazardous environments while improving industrial testing efficiency.
---

## **Problem**
Traditional heat spot testing requires manual repositioning of heating equipment inside a sealed chamber. This presents several issues:

- Safety risk due to extreme temperatures (-30°C to 100°C)
- Energy inefficiency from repeated chamber opening
- Time-consuming workflow

---

## **Solution**
I designed a **remotely controlled 2-axis motion system** that allows operators to precisely reposition the heating lamp from outside the chamber.

The system eliminates the need for human interaction inside extreme environments while maintaining testing accuracy.

---

## **System Design**
### Mechanical Design
- Custom rail structure resistant to extreme temperatures
- Lamp holder and mounting system
- Structural frame designed and validated using CAD analysis

### Control System
- Stepper motors with drivers for precise positioning
- Motion controller connected to a PC interface
- Custom GUI for real-time control

### Tools & Technologies
- Autodesk Inventor (mechanical design & analysis)
- Motion control system (stepper motor + driver)
- Custom control GUI

---

## **Key Features**
- Remote operation from outside the chamber
- High precision positioning using stepper motors
- Temperature-resilient mechanical design
- Reduced energy loss and improved workflow efficiency

---

## **Outcome**
- Eliminated direct human exposure to hazardous environments
- Improved testing efficiency by reducing setup time
- Maintained stable chamber conditions during operation
