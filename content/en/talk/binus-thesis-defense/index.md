abstract: "This presentation introduces the design and development of a remote-controlled heat spot testing system, created to address safety and efficiency challenges in industrial testing environments. The work focuses on replacing manual lamp positioning inside high-temperature chambers with a remotely operated mechanism. The system integrates mechanical structure design, a two-axis motion platform, and a control interface that enables precise positioning from outside the chamber. Key design considerations include thermal resistance, load capacity, motion accuracy, and system reliability. Experimental validation demonstrates stable operation across a wide temperature range and consistent positioning performance. This project highlights the application of mechanical design, control systems, and system integration in developing practical industrial solutions."
