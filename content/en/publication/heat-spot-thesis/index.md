abstract: Heat testing at a specific point on a tested object is conducted using an infrared lamp inside a heating chamber with a temperature capable of reaching 100°C. However, the lamp’s positioning is currently manually adjusted by humans, thus a device that can remotely control the lamp’s position from outside the heating chamber is necessary. The objective of this project is to design and create a frame and a remote-control system that can support the weight of the lamps, move the lamps in two axes, and operate at temperatures up to 100°C. The created device is then tested for load capacity, temperature resistance, distance, and speed in each movement direction. The results obtained show that the device can be remotely controlled and consistently reaches the designated distance target when operating within the temperature range of -30°C to 100°C with a load capacity up to 3kg. The achieved speed is approximately 95-100% of the set speed, with a maximum speed of 50mm/s.
